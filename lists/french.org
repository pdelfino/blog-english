This is my personal French survival cheat sheet. It helped me a lot
when I moved to Montreal. The "pronunciation" column is not real
phonetics --- it's a hack: how a Brazilian would read it out loud to
sound approximately correct in French. Nasalized vowels (~ã~, ~ẽ~,
~õ~) are marked explicitly; the rest you can read with your normal
Portuguese mouth.

*Legend:* =FR= = French | =PT= = Portuguese translation | =FALA= = read this in Portuguese and you'll be close enough.

*Tips before you start:*
- French =r= is guttural, like the =R= in /Rio/ or /rato/ in most of Brazil.
- French =u= (as in /tu/) has no equivalent in Portuguese. Round your lips like you're going to say "u" but try to say "i". When in doubt, say "ü".
- The =on= / =om= sound is nasal like "õ" but rounder.
- The =an= / =en= sound is nasal like "ã".
- Final consonants are usually silent, except =c=, =r=, =f=, =l= (think: *CaReFuL*).
- Liaison: when a word ends in a silent consonant and the next starts with a vowel, you link them.
